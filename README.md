# Electricity Distribution Performance
This project analyzes electricity distribution performance to evaluate energy supply, billing and revenue efficiency across different service areas.

## Company Context
A regional electricity distribution company is responsible for delivering reliable power to residential, commercial, and industrial customers through a network of substations and distribution lines.

The company requires a data driven dashboard to track electricity distribution performance, billing system, and revenue effeciency to support operational decision making.

## Business Questions
- Distribution efficiencies across service zones
- Performance (in terms of revenue) trends over time
- Regions driving the highest revenue loss
- How does payment behaviour differ across customers (residential, industrial, commercial)

## KPIs
- Total Energy Supplied (MWh)
- Total Amount Billed (in Naira)
- Total Revenue (in Naira)
- Collection Efficiency %
- Energy Billed (MWh)
- Revenue Leakage (in naira)

## Insights by Dimension
#### Regional Performance
The **Central region** recorded the highest revenue loss at **₦471M**, indicating it contributes the most to financial losses within the distribution network. This is followed by the **East(₦465M)** and **South(₦458M)** regions, which also show significantly high loss levels.

The **West(₦447M)** and **North(₦430M)** regions recorded comparatively lower revenue losses, though the gap between regions remains relatively small, suggesting that revenue leakage is a system-wide issue rather than a localized problem.

#### Customer Payment Behaviour
Payment patterns differ significantly across customer segments

**Industrial customers** generally contribute a larger share of revenue due to higher electricity consumption, while **residential customers** and **commercial customers** represent a larger customer base but contribut quite smaller to **Industrial customers**

---
## Recommendations
1. Management should prioritise the **Central region** to ensure Billing accuracies.
2. Industrial customers record the highest collection efficiency at **27.4%**, slightly outperfoming other customer segments. Even though they had **27.4%** collection efficiency, this indicates that revenue recovery is very low.
3. Collection efficiency across all customer segments (Residential, Industrial, Commercial) should be improved on for better improvement of payment structure.
